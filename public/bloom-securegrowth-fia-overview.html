Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Fixed Indexed Annuity?

A fixed indexed annuity is an insurance contract that credits interest based on the performance of a market index (such as the S&P 500) while protecting your principal from market losses. Your contract value can grow when the index performs well, but can never decline due to negative index performance thanks to the 0% floor.

How does index crediting work?

Interest is credited annually based on the change in the selected market index over each contract year. Depending on your chosen strategy, gains are subject to a cap rate, participation rate, or spread. If the index declines, no interest is credited (0%), but your contract value is never reduced. You are not directly invested in the index.

Is my principal protected?

Yes. Your initial premium is protected from market-related loss. The 0% floor means that in any contract year where the linked index has negative performance, your credited interest for that year is zero ‚Äî your contract value does not decrease. Note that withdrawals and rider fees will reduce contract value.

What is a surrender charge?

A surrender charge is a declining fee applied to withdrawals that exceed the annual free withdrawal allowance (10% of contract value) during the surrender period. The charge schedule starts at 9% in year one and declines to 0% after year ten. After the surrender period, your full contract value is available without penalty.

How does the income rider work?

The optional Guaranteed Lifetime Withdrawal Benefit (GLWB) rider provides a guaranteed annual withdrawal for life once activated. Your benefit base ‚Äî which determines your income amount ‚Äî grows at a guaranteed rollup rate during the deferral period. The longer you defer, the higher your guaranteed income. Rider fees of 0.95% are deducted annually from contract value.

What happens if I die during the surrender period?

Your named beneficiary will receive the death benefit ‚Äî typically the greater of the current contract value or the total premiums paid minus withdrawals. Surrender charges are waived at death. If the Enhanced Death Benefit Rider is elected, the benefit equals the highest anniversary value recorded.

Are gains taxed annually?

No. Interest credited to your annuity contract grows tax-deferred ‚Äî you do not owe income tax on gains until you take withdrawals. For non-qualified contracts, withdrawals are taxed on a last-in, first-out (LIFO) basis, meaning gains are withdrawn and taxed first. Withdrawals prior to age 59¬Ω may be subject to a 10% IRS penalty.

Can I add more money after the initial premium?

SecureGrowth Fixed Indexed Annuity is a single-premium product. Additional deposits are not accepted after the initial premium is applied. If you have additional funds to allocate, your Bloom agent can help you evaluate a second contract or other suitable options within your retirement plan.
