Frequently Asked Questions

What is a beneficiary?

Your beneficiary is the person ‚Äî or persons ‚Äî you designate to receive the death benefit proceeds. You may name multiple primary and contingent beneficiaries and update them at any time with a revocable designation.

How are claims paid?

Upon the death of the insured, your named beneficiary files a claim with Bloom Insurance. A tax-free lump-sum payment is issued directly to the beneficiary ‚Äî no income tax in most cases.

How much coverage do I need?

Coverage needs vary by individual and change over time. As a general guideline, most financial professionals recommend at least 10√ó your gross annual income. Our licensed agents can help you calculate the right amount.

I already have group life at work. Do I need more?

Group term coverage through your employer is a valuable benefit, but it typically follows you only while employed and may not be sufficient to cover long-term obligations. Whole life provides permanent, portable protection you own outright.

What is the cash value and when can I use it?

Cash value accumulates within your policy tax-deferred from the day coverage begins. You may borrow against it at any time for any purpose ‚Äî retirement income, a down payment, or emergencies ‚Äî without surrendering your coverage.

Will my premiums ever increase?

No. SecureLife Whole Life Premier features level premiums for the life of the policy. The rate established at issue is locked in permanently, regardless of age or any future changes in your health.
